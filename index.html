The heart of Camp Doodle

Copper

Before there was a Camp Doodle, there was Copper.

Copper has been by my side for almost eight years, through more miles, hotel rooms, job sites, and life changes than most dogs ever see. He is not just the father of this litter. He is my road dog, my shadow, my best good friend, and one of the biggest reasons Camp Doodle means what it means.

When Copper was still just a pup, life tested him early. At only eight months old, he was hit by a car and spent three months with casts on his front legs. During that time, he had to be carried outside, helped through every little routine, and loved through every hard day. That experience changed the bond between us. Copper learned to trust completely, and I learned just how much heart one dog could have.

After he healed, Copper went everywhere with me. I traveled for contract work from state to state, often living out of hotels, and Copper was right there for all of it. He has seen more of this country than a lot of people have — long drives, new places, strange rooms, early mornings, late nights, and every kind of day in between. Through it all, he stayed steady, loyal, and happy just to be with his person.

That is the kind of dog Copper is. Calm. Patient. People-loving. Tough when he had to be, gentle when it mattered most, and loyal in a way you cannot teach.

The puppies in this litter do not all look like Copper, and that is part of what makes them special. But what I hope they carry from him is even better than color — his trust, his sweetness, his steady nature, and that natural desire to stay close to the people they love.

Copper is the heart behind Camp Doodle. He has lived a big life, loved hard, and given us puppies that were raised around the kind of loyalty, patience, and companionship every family hopes to find in a dog.

Helpful details

Common questions

How do we get on the waitlist?

Send an inquiry below with your timing, preferred breed, and a little about your home. We will follow up with current plans and availability.

Can we choose a specific puppy?

Selections work best when temperament and family fit guide the decision. We will help you find the right match — and the quiz above is a great starting point.

What should we ask before reserving?

Ask about parent dogs, health records, go-home timing, coat type expectations, and what support looks like after pickup. We will answer every question honestly.

Where are you located?

We are a home-based breeder. Location details and pickup logistics are shared directly with families after initial contact.

What is included at pickup?

Health records, vaccination documentation, a care guide, feeding notes, and a familiar-scented item to help your puppy settle in.

Do you ship puppies?

We prefer in-person pickup so families can meet their puppy before going home. Reach out to discuss your situation and we will explore what works best.
